# Comprehensive Fundamental Analysis Report: Fastly, Inc. (FSLY)
## Date: April 5, 2026

## Company Profile
Fastly, Inc. is a cloud computing services company operating in the Technology sector, specifically classified in the Software - Application category. The company provides edge cloud computing, content delivery network (CDN), and related services to businesses across various industries. Fastly positions itself as a modern, high-performance cloud platform that enables developers to build and scale applications with reduced latency and improved user experiences.

## Financial Statement Analysis

### Balance Sheet Analysis
Fastly has demonstrated significant balance sheet transformation over the past four years (2022-2025):

**Debt Management Improvement:**
- Total debt has shown a major reduction from $833.5M in 2022 to $430.2M in 2025, representing a 48% decrease
- Net debt declined even more substantially from $561.3M to $181.3M over the same period
- This debt reduction reflects strategic financial discipline and improved cash flow generation

**Liquidity Position:**
- Cash position strengthened from $143.4M in 2022 to $180.6M by year-end 2025
- Working capital improved steadily: $484M (2022) → $315.9M (2023) → $335.7M (2024) → $312.7M (2025)
- Current ratio of 2.61 in 2025 indicates strong short-term liquidity management

**Asset Composition:**
- Total assets peaked at $1.9B in 2022 but have consolidated to $1.5B by 2025
- Goodwill remains significant at $670.4M, representing approximately 45% of total assets
- Intangible assets total $69.6M (2025)
- Net tangible assets grew to $233.5M in 2025, up from $76.1M at the bottom of the pandemic period in 2022

**Equity Structure:**
- Common stock equity showed minor fluctuations, ending 2025 at $929.6M
- Additional paid-in capital decreased from $1.66B (2022) to $2.04B (2025) due to stock repurchases
- Retained earnings turned negative in 2025 at -$1.11B, reflecting cumulative losses over the period

### Income Statement Analysis
Revenue Growth Momentum:
- **Strong revenue trajectory:** $432.7M (2022) → $506.0M (2023) → $543.7M (2024) → $624.0M (2025)
- **Revenue growth rate:** 44% CAGR over 2022-2025, with acceleration from 16.9% (2023) to 14.6% (2024) and 14.7% (2025)
- **Gross margin expansion:** Improved from 48.4% (2022) to 57.0% (2025), reflecting operational efficiency and pricing power

**Cost Structure Analysis:**
- Cost of revenue: $222.9M to $267.8M (45% growth over 4 years)
- R&D expenses maintained high level of investment: $155.3M → $162.7M
- SG&A expenses increased: $300.7M → $312.1M
- Stock-based compensation remained elevated: $145.8M → $117.3M

**Profitability Challenges:**
- **Net income deterioration:** Despite revenue growth, net losses have widened from -$190.8M (2022) to -$121.7M (2025)
- **Operating margin pressure:** Operating loss widened from -$246.2M (2022) to -$119.0M (2025)
- **Net margin decline:** Increased from negative to more negative at -19.5% (2025)
- **Gross margin expansion:** The 57% gross margin achieved in 2025 represents significant competitive positioning, offset by continued operational losses

**Non-Recurring Items:**
- $415M goodwill impairment charge in 2025 (non-cash, affects asset values but not cash flow)
- Total unusual items of $626K (2025) vs $54.4M (2022), indicating relative stabilization

### Cash Flow Statement Analysis

**Operating Cash Flow Turnaround:**
- Major improvement in operating cash flow: -$69.6M (2022) → $362K (2023) → $16.4M (2024) → $94.4M (2025)
- Operating cash flow turnaround occurred in 2024, reflecting improved working capital management

**Free Cash Flow Progression:**
- **Free cash flow improved significantly:** -$149.9M (2022) → -$31.9M (2023) → -$20.8M (2024) → $48.1M (2025)

- Represents a material improvement in cash generation capability

**Investing Activities:**
- Capital expenditure investment fluctuated: -$80.3M (2022) → -$36.2M (2023) → -$37.2M (2024) → -$46.4M (2025)
- Net investment purchase and sale positive in 2023 and 2025 due to investment liquidations
- Net PPE investment has shown steady pattern across years

**Financing Activities:**
- Strong balance sheet improvements: 2022: -$189.1M net financing; 2023: -$331.4M; 2024: -$17.1M; 2025: $12.8M
- 2025 represents first year of net positive financing cash flow
- Net issuance and payments of debt turned positive in 2025 via $180M short-term debt issuance
- Common stock payments: -$10.7M in 2023, reflecting potential share repurchases
- Proceeds from stock option exercises: $10.7M (2023) → $8.1M (2024)

**Interest Expense Management:**
- **Interest expense decreased:** $5.89M (2022) → $0.29M (2023) → $2.75M → $12.70M (2025)

1. **Revenue Growth Story Still Drives Narrative:** The 44% CAGR revenue growth remains the most compelling fundamental story for FSLY.

2. **Valuation vs. Execution Gap:** The market is pricing in a rapid path to profitability, with forward P/E at 92x despite current net losses.

3. **Cash Flow Turnaround Critical:** The turnaround from negative operating cash flow to $94M in 2025 and free cash flow of $48M is a positive development.

4. **Debt Reduction Creditworthy:** 48% reduction in total debt and $561M reduction in net debt reflect disciplined capital allocation over four years.

5. **Operating Efficiency Gains:** Gross margin expansion from 48.4% to 57% demonstrates improving competitive positioning.

6. **Investor Sentiment Indicator:** Share repurchases in 2023 and trading near 52-week highs suggest positive institutional confidence.

7. **High Growth Industry Context:** Edge computing and content delivery are growing sectors, potentially justifying premium valuation.

8. **Valuation Risk Premium:** Investors face execution risk with high P/E and P/B ratios that may not be justified if profitability timeline extends.

9. **Interest Rate Environment:** Net interest position improved; management appears to have navigated higher rates effectively.

10. **Long-Term Value Creation Potential:** High R&D investment suggests technology advantage that could drive future profitability.

The company has achieved material operational improvements, particularly in gross margin, cash generation, and debt reduction. However, the transition from loss-making to profitable operations is not yet realized despite impressive revenue growth.
