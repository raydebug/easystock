### **2. Rationale**

I am aligning with the **Aggressive Analyst's core conviction (Buy)** but must significantly amend the **Risk Management strategy (Stop-Loss)** based on the critique from the Conservative and Neutral Analysts.

**Critical Adjustments to the Plan (Learning from Mistakes):**
However, the **Aggressive Analyst's stop-loss suggestion ($18.87)** is dangerously flawed. The Conservative Analyst is correct: in a parabolic melt-up with an ATR of ~7.8%, a tight stop-loss below the moving average acts as a magnet for forced selling. A 40% drop could crush a stop-loss order during a "whipsaw," selling shares into a panic while the fundamental thesis remains intact (a common mistake in high-beta trading). We cannot "chase" the melt-up with a "hair-trigger" exit.

**Counterargument Addressed:**
The Conservative Analyst warns of a crash to $11.17. While they are fearful of the short term, the Aggressive Analyst correctly identifies that the *trend* has broken to the upside. However, we should not bet the entire farm on a jump to infinity. We take the upside but protect against the volatility trap.

Therefore, **Buy** is the thesis, but the execution must be disciplined to avoid the "chop" cited by the Neutral Analyst.
