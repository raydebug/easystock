# SUPER MICRO COMPUTER (SMCI) - COMPREHENSIVE ANALYSIS REPORT
## Date: March 22, 2026

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

SMCI has experienced what could be a transformative negative event after federal prosecutors indicted co-founder Wally Liaw for allegedly orchestrating an illegal scheme to smuggle billions in high-end AI servers containing Nvidia chips to China. The stock has crashed 33% amid widespread concern about regulatory compliance, potential S&P 500 exclusion, supply chain disruptions, and the company's reputation as a bellwether in the AI hardware space.

---

## 1. KEY DEVELOPMENTS - THE SMUGGLING SCANDAL

### 1.1 Federal Indictment Details
U.S. prosecutors have unsealed criminal charges against:
- **Wally Liaw** - Co-founder and former board member
- **Ruei-Tsang Chang** - Sales manager
- **Ting-Wei Sun** - Contractor

The trio allegedly conspired to route **$2.5 billion in AI servers** containing "banned" Nvidia chips to China, violating U.S. export control laws.

### 1.2 Co-Founder Resignation
Liaw has resigned from the SMCI board following his indictment. Super Micro confirmed this move, indicating a clean break from the accused individuals while maintaining that the company cooperates with authorities.
