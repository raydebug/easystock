## Income Statement Detailed Analysis

### Revenue Analysis
TSLA has shown consistent, albeit non-linear, revenue generation across its fiscal quarters. The Q3 2025 peak ($28.1B) is followed by a 11% revenue decline in Q4 2025 ($24.9B). This pattern may reflect seasonality or product mix shifts. Notably, Q2 revenue ($22.5B) was below Q1 2025 ($19.3B).

### Cost Structure Analysis
Tesla maintains a vertically integrated business model with substantial investment in Research & Development ($1.43–1.78B per quarter) and Selling, General, & Administrative expenses ($1.36–1.65B per quarter). These combined operating expenses total approximately $3.0–3.5B per quarter.

### Operating Margin and Earnings
Operating income remained relatively stable and modest in absolute terms:

- Q4 2025: $1.41B
- Q3 2025: $1.62B
- Q1 2025: $3.99B

The company posted a net income of $840M in Q4 2025, representing a return on revenue of approximately 3.4%. Net income varied significantly by quarter as a result of unusual items and cost structure, which can create earnings volatility despite the company’s overall scale.

### Share Count
Average diluted shares (TTM): ≈3.54B. This figure is slightly higher than prior-year levels; combined with lower net income in Q4 2025, it contributed to the diluted EPS of $0.24.
