# TSLA Technical Analysis Report (April 9, 2026)

## Executive Summary

Based on analysis across 8 technical indicators spanning March 10-April 8, 2026, TSLA is experiencing a **moderate correction phase** following a major rally. The stock has consolidated within a tightening trading range, with momentum indicators showing improving conditions. The technical picture suggests a potential bottoming pattern forming, with current levels providing support.

## Detailed Technical Analysis

### 1. Trend Analysis - Medium to Long-Term Bullish

**50-Day SMA (Medium-Term Trend):**
- **Data**: Declined from 424.55 on March 10 to 397.63 on April 8
- **Analysis**: The 50-day moving average shows a downward trajectory over the past month, indicating a short-term pullback from recent highs. This normalizes after the strong rally to maintain alignment with price action. The SMA slope suggests the pullback is moderate and not severe given recent historical performance.
- **Key Insight**: The downward slope is a trend correction, not a reversal, especially when viewed in context of prior strong multi-month uptrends.

**200-Day SMA (Long-Term Trend):**
- **Data**: Rose from 392.70 on March 10 to 397.32 on April 8
- **Analysis**: The 200-day SMA has maintained an uptrend throughout the period, serving as a steady anchor. It sits below the 50-day SMA in the current context, supporting a continuing secular uptrend.

**Trend Status**: **MEDIUM-TERM BULLISH WITH CORRECTION**
- The long-term trend remains intact due to the 200-day SMA's upward trajectory
- A temporary pullback is occurring, which is healthy for price action following a steep rally
- The pullback is normalizing before potential continuation.

### 2. Momentum Analysis - Improving Bearish Pressure

**MACD Line:**

- **Analysis**: Negative MACD values signify short-term bearish momentum. The downward movement indicates momentum is bearish, but the decline (i.e., becoming more negative) has been moderate and consistent with volatility in volatile stock profiles. Combined with the tightening bands and RSI in oversold territory, this suggests a corrective phase rather than an accelerating decline.

**MACD Signal:**
- **Data**: Declined from -7.30 to -11.79 over the same period
- **Analysis**: The signal line's descent reflects consistent bearish momentum. It has been tracking closely with the MACD line, confirming that momentum is negative across this window.

**MACD Histogram:**

- **Analysis**: The histogram peaked in mid-March, then moved negative, suggesting momentum shifted and became bearish. The increase in negative height (from ~0.09 on March 10 to ~-2.49 on April 8) indicates accelerating bearish momentum since around March 19 when the peak occurred.

**Momentum Status**: **WEAK BEARISH MOMENTUM**
- Both MACD lines and histogram remain negative, indicating overall sell pressure
- The increase in negative histogram values suggests momentum weakness has intensified since around March 19
- There is no clear bullish crossover pattern in place; the bearishness persists as of the current window

### 3. Price Action & Volatility - Consolidation Phase

**Bollinger Bands:**
- **Upper Band**: Contracted from 427.40 on March 10 to 413.38 on April 8
- **Lower Band**: Declined from 389.91 on March 10 to 339.00 on April 8
- **Middle Band (20 SMA)**: Ranged roughly between 350-410
- **Analysis**: The bands are contracting significantly, particularly the lower band's decline. This compressive action often precedes a breakout or a continuation of the prevailing direction. With prices generally inside the bands, there is no confirmed breakout but rather a consolidative, corrective phase within a tight range. The lower band's downward move aligns with a pullback in price, as TSLA is reacting from the upper end.

**Price Action Context**:
- The lower band's sharp decline in April indicates a significant support test as prices dipped in early April
- Prices remain above the lower band into early April, which typically reflects some ongoing support during the pullback
- The contraction is a natural response to the rally, allowing the indicators to adjust to the new price zone

**Volatility Status**: **MODERATE VOLATILITY, CONTRACTING**
- Band contraction suggests less extreme volatility as a correction phase unfolds; a re-expansion could follow a breakout

### 4. Oversold/Overbought Condition - Potential Bottom Formation

**RSI (14-Day):**
- **Data**: Currently at 33.26 on April 8 (from 31.75 on March 31)
- **Analysis**: The RSI has moved above its late-March low but remains below 35. The recent dip to about 31.75 suggests the stock dipped near oversold before a mild bounce, which is consistent with a stabilization bottom formation rather than a continuous downtrend. Since early March, the RSI has been in the 30s, with some spikes in the 40s and 50s, reflecting a corrective trading range rather than a continuous selloff.
- **Range Context**: The current RSI around 33 is in oversold territory (typically <35), indicating the pullback is nearing a potential exhaustion point.

### 5. Trading Range & Support/Resistance Observations

**Key Price Levels (Recent Trends):**
- **Major Resistance Zone**: Upper band in the 410+ range, reflecting prior highs
- **Current Consolidation Zone**: Broadly between the middle band and lower band (~350-410)
- **Key Support**: Lower band around 340, reflecting the most recent low in this window

### 6. Risk vs. Opportunity Assessment

**Current Level Context**:
- The stock has pulled back within the context of a continuing uptrend as supported by the rising 200-day SMA and prior strong multi-month performance.
- Support levels around the lower band and the 200-day SMA provide cushioning during the pullback, reducing the probability of a full trend reversal.
- The low RSI indicates some exhaustion in the sell-off, suggesting the pullback may be nearing a bottoming area.

**Positioning Considerations**:
- If positioned long, this correction provides an opportunity to enter or add positions at more favorable levels, with stop-losses above the recent swing highs.
- For those scaling in, support near the lower band (~340) and the 200-day SMA (~397) may represent areas where the pullback could stabilize before potential continuation.
- A cautious approach is advisable given the current bearish momentum indicators and the need for confirmation of a bottom before aggressive upside entry.

### 7. Conclusion

TSLA is undergoing a moderate correction within a continuing uptrend, as evidenced by the rising 200-day SMA and prior multi-month rally. The current pullback has tightened over the past week, with key support around the lower band (near ~340) and the 200-day SMA (~397). Momentum remains weakly bearish, with the RSI just above oversold territory. This setup may represent a stabilization/reaction phase rather than a full trend reversal, offering risk-managed opportunities for traders to participate.
