## Global Macro and Market Analysis Report | March 21-28, 2026

### Executive Summary
The past week (March 21-28, 2026) presents a challenging market environment characterized by heightened volatility, mixed sector performance, and significant concerns around trade and supply chain disruptions impacting key technology stocks. While AI-driven tech growth narratives continue to resonate, companies with exposure to international trade face headwinds.

**Bullish Trends:**
- **Marvell Technology** stands out as a market mover with strong data center revenue growth, reportedly up 21% in recent periods. Analysts project significant upside potential (50% in 2026), driven by AI infrastructure demand
- **Credo Technology Group** is gaining traction due to growing demand for AI interconnect solutions

**AI/Infrastructure Drivers:**

- PCIe and interconnect technologies emerging as critical enablers for AI workloads

### Trading Implications

**Short-Term Opportunities & Risks:**

1. **Marvell (MRVL)** - Appears to be the most actionable tech stock narrative in the current environment, driven by:
   - Strong data center revenue growth (21% mentioned)
   - AI fundamentals intact despite macro headwinds
   - Professional analyst support projecting 50% upside

2. **Diversified Exposure Concerns** - The volatility seen in peers suggests selective rather than broad portfolio approaches to tech exposure

3. **Trade Policy Risk Premium** - Companies with global supply chains are effectively pricing in additional uncertainty, potentially dampening near-term multiple expansion despite strong fundamentals

**Medium-Term Outlook:**
- AI infrastructure buildout appears to have legs, with semiconductor vendors positioned to benefit
- Companies successfully navigating supply chain constraints and trade risks should outperform
- Market rotation toward quality and dividend-paying names may persist given macro uncertainty

4. **Global Economic Growth**: Broader macro weakness could reduce discretionary IT spending

### Sector Rotation Signals
- Tech remains the primary growth driver but with heightened volatility
