## Macroeconomic Trends

### Geopolitical Instability Dominates Risk Premium

The "short-war theory" narrative has completely collapsed according to Barrons and Fortune analysis. With the Dow entering a tailspin and the Strait of Hormuz remaining a critical bottleneck, investors have abandoned the expectation of a brief conflict. This creates:
- Elevated risk premia across equity markets
- Increased flight-to-safety positioning in cash, bonds, and defensive assets
- Discretionary trading behavior replacing algorithmic momentum

### Trade & Supply Chain Risks Emerge as Structural Concern

Multiple sources highlight emerging trade and supply chain vulnerabilities, particularly in:
- Semiconductor manufacturing distribution
- International logistics through conflict-impacted corridors
- Digital goods classification under evolving WTO frameworks

The stalemate in WTO e-commerce moratorium discussions suggests digital trade policies remain contentious, potentially creating regulatory uncertainty for tech platforms and AI services.

### Market Concentration Risks Elevated

A critical observation from 24/7 Wall St.: SPY's top 3 holdings now control market outcome. As passive investing dominates, this concentration creates compounding downside risk - when the largest assets fail, the index suffers disproportionately. This structure favors active management and tactical position selection over pure passive strategies.

- Historical valuation compression not seen since early 2020s crises

UBS maintained a Buy rating despite lowering price target from $600 to $510, indicating:
- Fundamental valuation disconnect relative to current price

- Analyst confidence in long-term positioning despite macro risks

**4. Diversified AI Revenue Streams**
