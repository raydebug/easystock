FINANCIAL MARKETS ANALYSIS REPORT: NFLX
Date: 2026-04-11

## EXECUTIVE OVERVIEW

Netflix (NFLX) has undergone a dramatic technical transformation over the past six months, transitioning from a strong uptrend to extended selling pressure, and now appears to be stabilizing with emerging bullish momentum. The stock currently trades at $103.01, having stabilized after a severe correction that saw the price drop approximately 47% from its January 21st high of $85.36 to a February 12th low of $75.86.

## DETAILED ANALYSIS

### 1. LONG-TERM TREND (200 SMA)
The 200-day exponential moving average ($106.54 as of April 10) serves as the primary trend reference. This SMA has been on a consistent downward trajectory throughout the reporting period, descending from $113.40 in early December through $111.08 in late October and now closing in on $100. The key observations reveal:

- **Structural Downtrend Confirmation**: The 200 SMA is trading below the current price level, suggesting the broader uptrend from pre-October is still intact but under significant stress
- **Bullish Potential**: As prices trade above a declining 200 SMA, this positions NFLX for a potential "golden cross" setup when the slower moving average reaches a trough and begins turning upward, offering the strongest long-term confirmation signal
- **Support Zone**: The long-term baseline remains a critical reference point for valuation and trend assessment

### 2. SHORT-TERM MOMENTUM (10 EMA)
The 10-day exponential moving average has rallied from $93.99 to $98.68 over the past week, demonstrating strong short-term bullish pressure. This fast-acting line indicates:

- **Bullish Momentum**: The continued upward trajectory suggests buyer interest in recovery attempts, though the gap between 10 EMA and price ($103.01) indicates room for both continued consolidation or further upside
- **Momentum Buildup**: The steeper slope of the 10 EMA compared to slower moving averages suggests increasing buying pressure, potentially leading to continued short-term rally attempts
- **Volume Confirmation**: The recent price action shows increased volume on both sides of the volatility, with notable spikes around Feb 27 ($200.8M) and recent April consolidation days ($30M+ volume), supporting the sustainability of the current momentum structure

### 3. MEDIUM-TERM TREND (50 SMA)
The 50-day exponential moving average currently sits at $89.70, having declined significantly from $122.14 in October. This indicator reveals:

- **Downtrend Resilience**: Despite recent price rallies, the 50 SMA remains below the current price, confirming the underlying medium-term trend remains challenged
- **Potential Trend Flip**: If price action can sustain trading above the 50 SMA with continued bullish momentum, a trend reversal could be validated in the coming weeks
- **Support Dynamics**: The 50 SMA serves as a dynamic support/resistance level, having previously provided key levels during the December correction, with a potential retest of this area depending on continuation or reversal of current price action

### 4. MOMENTUM OSCILLATOR (MACD)
The MACD has shown remarkable improvement, with the MACD line rising from -0.41 in early March to +2.745 as of April 10, accompanied by a corresponding increase in the MACD signal line from -0.23 to +2.116. The MACD histogram has expanded from negative territory (-0.318 on March 31) to positive readings (+0.629), indicating:

- **Bullish Momentum Shift**: This represents a complete reversal from the bearish divergence and negative momentum observed throughout the January correction period, suggesting renewed buyer interest
- **Continued Strength**: The expanding gap between MACD and signal lines indicates accelerating momentum, potentially suggesting continued strength in the current rally phase
- **Potential Exhaustion Point**: As the gap widens, traders should monitor for potential convergence or divergence patterns that could signal a change in momentum direction

### 5. TREND STRENGTH (RSI)
The Relative Strength Index has shown strong recovery, rising from extreme oversold readings of 37.95 on April 1 to 72.67 as of April 10. This represents:

- **Oversold Correction**: The move from oversold territory followed the 40% decline in price, representing a typical correction phase in a volatile medium-term trend
- **Bullish Recovery**: The rally in RSI from March lows (48.74) of 55.38 to current 72.67 levels demonstrates strong buyer participation in the recent bounce attempt
- **Potential Overbought Conditions**: The proximity above the 70 threshold suggests some momentum might be extending into the overbought range, though a continuation suggests either continued buying or a transition from trend-driven to mean-reversion trading patterns

### 6. VOLATILITY & SUPPORT (Bollinger Bands)
The 20-day simple moving average (middle band) currently sits at approximately $95.60, with the current price of $103.01 positioned well above this level. This indicates:

- **Breakout Potential**: Prices trading above the middle band with sustained momentum could lead to test of the upper band ($105-108), representing the next technical resistance zone
- **Mean Reversion Risk**: As prices extend above key moving averages, the risk of mean reversion to the 50-60 band range temporarily increases, requiring careful position management
- **Support Zone**: The middle band area ($95-100) as a potential stabilization zone if prices pull back from current levels, offering a test of buyer commitment and sentiment

### 7. RECORDED TRADING DYNAMICS
- January 21-26: Sustained volatility with $85.36 to $77.20 swings, high volume days on both sides
- February 12: Extreme volatility with low price $75.86 and high vol day with $200.8M volume
- Recent March: Consolidations and rallies, with volume spikes around March 25-27 supporting the recovery attempt

## STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS

The combination of these technical indicators suggests Netflix has completed a major correction phase and is now attempting to establish a new intermediate-term trend. The recovery from February lows with strengthened MACD, rising RSI, and price action above short-term moving averages indicates a potential trend reversal setup, though caution is warranted given the continued headwind of prices below the 50 and 200 SMAs.

## RECOMMENDATION

Based on the comprehensive technical analysis, Netflix appears to be in a transitional phase between a medium-term downtrend and a potential new uptrend formation. The current setup offers both risk and opportunity, with prices demonstrating resilient buyer interest despite the challenging environment.
