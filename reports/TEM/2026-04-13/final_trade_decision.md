### **1. Summary of Key Arguments**

The company is burning cash. The 50-Day SMA at $51.58 is likely overhead resistance, where selling pressure will resume. A "death spiral" is a real possibility if growth falters.
*   **The Moderate Position (Neutral Analyst):** The growth is real, but the leverage is scary. Chasing the "dip" is dangerous. The Stair-Step entry should be abandoned in favor of waiting for a **valid bottom** confirmed by volume, not just price action. The stock is currently floating between Bollinger bands ($41.88 - $52.09).

### **2. Rationale**

The debate highlights a classic **Value Trap vs. Opportunity** scenario. The Conservative Analyst’s fear of insolvency is valid if the revenue growth stalls, which is not currently reflected in the chart. However, the Aggressive Analyst’s dismissal of the balance sheet ignores the cash-on-hand which provides liquidity until the revenue matures.

*   **Why Buy?** The data moat in healthcare is high. The $1.47B revenue run rate is not "hope"—it is existing cash flow. A 6x multiple on *high-growth*, *high-margin* (64.7%) revenue is statistically cheaper than the current market average for AI stocks. The gap between the asset value and the stock price (the "dividend" to the buyer) is too large to ignore.
*   **Why Wait on Entry?** The Conservative Analyst correctly warns that buying a broken chart can lead to losing money on the entry itself. The Neutral Analyst’s suggestion to wait for a **close above the 50-Day SMA ($51.58) on high volume** is the "goldilocks" entry. It ensures we aren't chasing the knife but rather riding the trend *once* the capitulation has occurred.
