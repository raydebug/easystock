# Comprehensive Fundamental Analysis: Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSM)

## Company Overview

**Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company Limited** is the world's largest dedicated semiconductor foundry and a key player in the global technology supply chain. As a foundry, TSMC manufactures chips for various companies without competing directly with them. Operating in the Technology/Semiconductors sector, TSMC serves customers across consumer electronics, automotive, IoT, and high-performance computing industries.

**Market Position:** TSMC holds a dominant market share in advanced semiconductor manufacturing, particularly in nodes below 7nm, where it holds a near-monopoly position. This position makes it a critical component of the global tech ecosystem.

## Key Insights

1. **Strong Revenue and Earnings Growth**: TSM has shown consistent double-digit percentage revenue growth over the past year, driven by strong demand across multiple verticals including AI chips, automotive electronics, and consumer smartphones.

2. **High Capital Intensity**: The company maintains a very high capex-to-revenue ratio (~34%), with over $361 billion invested in Q4 2025. This reflects the capital-intensive nature of semiconductor manufacturing but also signals commitment to future growth and technological leadership.

3. **High profitability with strong margins**: The company maintains industry-leading operating margins of over 54%, benefiting from its position as the market leader in advanced node manufacturing.

4. **Strategic investment in R&D**: Research and development expenditures remain significant at $65+ billion annually, supporting its competitive advantage in next-generation semiconductor technology.

5. **Financial strength**: The company maintains a strong balance sheet with a current ratio above 2.6x, adequate liquidity, and healthy profitability metrics.

6. **Industry positioning**: TSMC is at the center of the semiconductor supply chain disruption, with increasing importance in critical technologies like AI, automotive, and high-performance computing.

## Risk Considerations

1. **Industry cyclicality**: Semiconductor industry is cyclical and faces potential downturns during macroeconomic slowdowns or inventory adjustments.

2. **Technological competition**: Continued investments in advanced nodes (N2, N3, etc.) require significant resources and face competition from foundry competitors.

3. **Geopolitical risks**: Location in Taiwan raises potential geopolitical exposure.

4. **Valuation**: TSM trades at a P/E of 35.46x (TTM, trailing) and 20.52x (forward), which may be elevated given cyclical industry exposure and growth expectations.

5. **Capital intensity**: High capex requirements may pressure free cash flow when demand softens.
