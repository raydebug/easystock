# Comprehensive SNAP Technical Analysis Report

## Executive Summary

SNAP is currently navigating a complex intermediate downtrend with mixed technical signals. The stock shows signs of stabilization following extreme oversold conditions but remains constrained below critical moving averages. Long-term momentum appears weak, while potential recovery attempts are still gathering strength.

---

## 1. Trend Analysis

### Long-Term Trend (Bearish)
**200 SMA Analysis**: The 200-day moving average has tracked a consistent multi-month downtrend from approximately $9.87 in late August 2025 to around $7.30 by early April 2026. This sustained decline over a full trading year indicates strong underlying bearish pressure, with the 200 SMA acting as a powerful resistance level that the stock has struggled to approach. The 200-day average's persistent downward slope underscores a fundamentally weak long-term trajectory.

### Medium-Term Trend (Weakening)
**50 SMA Analysis**: The 50-day moving average recently broke through a critical level near $5.00, transitioning from a declining curve to a more sustained trend. This breakdown from a medium-term uptrend around February suggests renewed downside acceleration. The current position below the 50-day average reinforces weakness, with the SMA now acting as a resistance barrier. Momentum appears to be weakening as the average enters a steeper decline phase.

### Short-Term Dynamics
Price has recently found support in the mid-$4.50 range, exhibiting a modest consolidation pattern with minor recovery attempts. This suggests the downside is being partially contained, but without clear directional confirmation from momentum metrics.

---

## 2. Momentum Analysis

### RSI: Oversold-to-Moderate Transition
RSI has transitioned from extreme oversold conditions around 17.40 in late February to approximately 57.58 by early April, showing a substantial bounce. This range suggests the stock has moved from distressed territory toward more normalized conditions, though it remains well below neutral territory (70+). The shift from sub-20 readings to sustained low-to-mid-50 levels indicates a stabilization phase, yet the absence of overbought readings (above 50 for multiple consecutive periods) warns that momentum remains subdued.

### MACD: Bearish Consistency
MACD has remained stuck in negative territory throughout the period, oscillating between approximately -0.78 (February lows) and -0.04 (early April highs). Both the MACD line (-0.04) and MACD signal line (-0.16) are currently below zero, highlighting persistent bearish momentum. The narrowing spread suggests waning downside pressure—but still not enough to generate bullish crossover signals. This consolidation phase in negative territory indicates cautious sentiment and lack of strong conviction from trend-following models.

---

## 3. Volatility Analysis

### Bollinger Bands: Contraction and Potential Breakout
Bollinger bands are showing clear signs of increased volatility and potential regime change. The Bollinger middle band has descended from ~$4.87 to ~$4.55 as the lower bound. The upper band, which has been declining into early March, has notably reversed with a distinct uptrend above recent highs, reaching around $5.23 by early April. This divergence—while still below the peak upper band near $5.56—signals that volatility may be expanding and the stock could be prepping for a break out. The gap between lower and upper bands has tightened significantly, indicating a compression phase that historically precedes directional moves.

### VWMA: Continued Downtrend Weighting
Volume-weighted moving average has declined from around $4.72 to $4.50, showing that volume-adjusted price action also remains in a downtrend. This metric highlights that despite recent stabilization attempts, sustained price movement still leans bearish once volume is factored in.

---

**Bullish Factors:**

**Bearish Factors:**
