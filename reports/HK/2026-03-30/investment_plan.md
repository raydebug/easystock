**Recommendation: SELL**
I am aligning with the Bear Analyst in this scenario. You cannot build a billion-dollar infrastructure empire on borrowed money when the cost of borrowing is skyrocketing due to inflation fears, and you are losing market share to a competitor that is essentially cutting your throat from the bottom.

The "Infrastructural" thesis relies on high CapEx, but in a recession, high CapEx is viewed as operational waste rather than future earnings. Additionally, losing share to BYD in Europe is a structural warning sign that the moat is eroding. The VIX is a feature, not a bug—it is screaming that the safe money is leaving the casino, and Tesla is sitting right in the middle of the table with a weak hand.
